Introduction

Welcome to my website! I am a geospatial analyst and remote sensing specialist with a master's in GIS, a bachelor's in data science, and experience in environmental research. My skills in Python, Google Earth Engine, and ArcGIS Pro support data-driven solutions for environmental and urban challenges. I use geospatial technologies to address sustainable development and community-focused projects. Make sure to view the Portfolio tab to take a look at some examples of my past work!

Education

I hold a Bachelor of Arts in Data Science and Geography from Clark University and am currently pursuing a Master’s degree in GIS through the Advanced Degree Program. As an undergraduate, my work focused on organizing and analyzing large remotely sensed datasets, such as Landsat and Sentinel imagery. I am currently an environmental GIS Consultant with past clients being the Massachusetts Department of Conservation and Recreation (DCR) and the Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS. During my studies I was the Teaching Assistant for the course Advanced Geospatial Analysis with Python, taught by Hamed Alemohamad (course materials available here). Previously, I served as a Course Assistant for Introduction to Society and Computing. Additionally, I participated in the Human Environment Research Observatory (HERO) Lab, where I analyzed tree health and survivorship across Worcester, MA, and surrounding towns. My Undergraduate thesis examined Urban Heat Islands and Environmental Justice in Worcester, MA, using remotely sensed data.

Research Focus

My research has primarily focused on remote sensing, employing a range of techniques and tools to analyze and process spatial data. These include machine learning, deep learning, APIs such as Stackstac and ArcPy, and Python libraries like Xarray and Geopandas. I have applied these skills predominantly to environmentally focused projects, with a significant portion of my research dedicated to studying urban heat islands. Additionally, I have worked on projects involving air quality analysis, remote detection and classification of agricultural areas, and urban forestry.
